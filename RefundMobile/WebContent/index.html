What you will need.

You filed your return, and you've waited the recommended time before checking on your refund status. Now you want to know when you will receive your refund. You will need to provide the following information from your 2013 Oregon return:

SSN or ITIN

Your Social Security number (SSN) or IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). For a joint return, you can use either filer's SSN or ITIN, and

Filing Status

Filing status (single, married filing jointly, registered domestic partners filing jointly (RDP), married filing separately, RDP filing separately, head of household, or qualifying widow(er), and

Refund Amount

The exact amount in whole dollars of the refund you requested on your return.

  • If you filed Form 40, enter the amount from line 48 (Overpayment line)
  • If you filed Form 40N, enter the amount from line 66 (Overpayment line)
  • If you filed Form 40P, enter the amount from line 66 (Overpayment line)
